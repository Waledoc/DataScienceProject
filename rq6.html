RQ6 — Correlation between Electricity Consumption and Generation in TSO Regions

To what extent is electricity consumption correlated with electricity generation within Germany’s TSO control areas, and are there observable relationships between regional demand and regional electricity production?

Research Focus

This analysis investigates how strongly electricity consumption and electricity generation are related within Germany’s TSO control areas and whether regional demand and regional production show observable structural relationships.

Key Results

The regional SMARD data from 2022 to 2025 shows a clear positive relationship between electricity consumption and electricity generation in all four TSO regions, although the strength of the relationship differs noticeably by zone.

The approximate correlation between daily electricity consumption and daily total electricity generation is r = 0.79 for 50Hertz, r = 0.69 for Amprion, r = 0.48 for TransnetBW, and r = 0.41 for TenneT.

This means that all four regions show a positive correlation, but the coupling between regional demand and regional production is clearly stronger in some areas than in others.

The data also suggests a structural pattern in which 50Hertz and TenneT appear closer to or above their regional demand levels on average, while Amprion and TransnetBW appear below their average consumption levels.

Interpretation

In every TSO region, higher electricity consumption tends to be associated with higher electricity generation. This indicates a general connection between regional demand and regional production.

The relationship is strongest in 50Hertz and Amprion, suggesting that in these areas generation tracks regional demand more closely.

In TenneT and TransnetBW, the correlation is weaker. This suggests that regional production is less tightly aligned with regional consumption on a day-to-day basis.

Overall, the results point to a broader spatial structure in the German electricity system, where some TSO areas are more generation-heavy and others more demand-heavy.

Summary: Electricity consumption and electricity generation are positively related in all TSO control areas, but the strength of this relationship varies considerably across regions.
