RQ1 — Public Holidays & Electricity Consumption

Do public holidays and school holidays in Germany show a measurable difference in daily electricity consumption compared to regular working days?

Research Focus

This analysis compares electricity consumption on regular working days, public holidays, school holiday periods and Sundays in Germany as a whole.

Key Results

The evaluation of the SMARD data from 2022 to 2025 combined with the holiday dataset shows a clear difference in electricity consumption between regular working days and holiday-related days.

Average electricity consumption is approximately 1.99 million MWh on holiday days, compared to around 2.15 million MWh on regular working days. This means that electricity consumption on holiday days is about 7.4 % lower than on regular working days.

In this dataset, Sundays show an average consumption of approximately 2.24 million MWh.

Interpretation

Electricity consumption in Germany is noticeably lower on public holidays and school holiday days than on regular working days.

A likely explanation is reduced industrial activity, lower commercial electricity demand, and the partial closure of offices, schools and public services during holiday periods.

The fact that Sundays show slightly higher consumption than holiday days may be related to the inclusion of extended school holiday periods in the dataset, which can create different demand patterns than typical public holidays.

Summary: Electricity consumption in Germany is about 7 % lower on holiday days than on regular working days, mainly due to reduced industrial and commercial activity.
