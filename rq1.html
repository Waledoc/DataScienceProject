RQ1 — Public Holidays & Electricity Consumption

Do public holidays and school holidays in Germany show a measurable difference in daily electricity consumption compared to regular working days?

Research Focus

This analysis compares electricity consumption on regular working days, public holidays, school holiday periods and Sundays in Germany as a whole.

Key Results

The analysis of the SMARD data from 2022 to 2025 combined with the holiday dataset shows clear differences in electricity consumption across day types.

Regular weekdays had the highest average electricity consumption at approximately 1.37 million MWh. Sundays showed the lowest average consumption at around 1.11 million MWh, while Saturdays averaged about 1.17 million MWh. Holiday days also had lower consumption than regular weekdays, with an average of approximately 1.21 million MWh.

Overall, this indicates that electricity consumption was highest on regular working days and reduced on weekends and holiday-related days.

Interpretation

Electricity consumption in Germany was noticeably lower on weekends and holiday-related days than on regular working days.

A likely explanation is reduced industrial activity, lower commercial electricity demand, and changes in everyday routines during these periods.

The particularly low consumption on Sundays suggests that reduced business activity has a strong influence on overall electricity demand.

Summary: Electricity consumption in Germany was highest on regular working days and lower on weekends and holiday-related days, mainly due to reduced industrial and commercial activity.
