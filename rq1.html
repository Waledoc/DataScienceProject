RQ1 — Public Holidays & Electricity Consumption

Do public holidays and school holidays in Germany show a measurable difference in daily electricity consumption compared to regular working days?

Research Focus

This section investigates whether electricity consumption in Germany differs systematically between regular working days, Saturdays, Sundays, and holiday days.

Key Results

Across all three visualizations, the results show clear differences in electricity consumption between regular weekdays, Saturdays, Sundays, and holiday days.

The bar chart shows that regular weekdays had the highest average electricity consumption at approximately 1.37 million MWh, while Sundays had the lowest average consumption at around 1.11 million MWh. Saturdays averaged about 1.17 million MWh, and holiday days were slightly higher at approximately 1.21 million MWh.

The boxplot confirms the same overall pattern in the distribution of the data, and the time series shows repeated drops during holiday periods while broader weekly and seasonal patterns remain visible. Overall, this indicates that electricity consumption was highest on regular working days and lower on weekends and holiday days.

Visualization 1 — Average Consumption by Day Type

This bar chart summarizes average electricity consumption for four day types in Germany. The horizontal categories distinguish between weekdays, Saturdays, Sundays, and holidays, while the vertical scale reports average consumption in MWh. The pattern is clear: weekdays record the highest average values, Sundays the lowest, and Saturdays and holidays fall between these two points.

Visualization 2 — Boxplot by Day Type

This boxplot provides a more detailed view of consumption across the same day types. The line inside each box marks the median, the box itself contains the middle 50 percent of observations, and the points outside represent outliers. The figure reinforces the same overall result as the first chart, showing that the differences between day types are visible not only in the averages but also in the broader distribution of the data.

Visualization 3 — Time Series during Holiday Periods

This time series traces electricity consumption over holiday periods across the full observation window. It reveals a pronounced weekly rhythm, with recurring declines on Sundays and other comparatively low-activity days. Holiday periods are also frequently associated with reduced consumption. At the same time, broader seasonal movements remain visible, including marked declines around Christmas and New Year as well as a noticeable summer dip in July and August.

Interpretation

Electricity consumption in Germany was noticeably lower on weekends and holiday days than on regular working days.

A likely explanation is reduced industrial activity, lower commercial electricity demand, and changes in everyday routines during these periods.

The particularly low consumption on Sundays suggests that reduced business activity has a strong influence on overall electricity demand.

Summary: Electricity consumption in Germany was highest on regular working days and lower on weekends and holiday days, mainly due to reduced industrial and commercial activity.
