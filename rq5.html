RQ5 — Regional Differences in Electricity Consumption

How does electricity consumption differ between Germany’s TSO control areas, and which factors may explain these regional differences?

Research Focus

This analysis compares electricity consumption across Germany’s four TSO control areas and examines which structural factors may explain the observed regional differences.

Key Results

The SMARD data from 2022 to 2025 shows clear regional differences in electricity consumption across the four TSO control areas. Amprion has the highest average electricity consumption, followed by TenneT, then 50Hertz, while TransnetBW has the lowest.

The approximate average daily electricity consumption is 459,000 MWh for Amprion, 387,000 MWh for TenneT, 279,000 MWh for 50Hertz, and 158,000 MWh for TransnetBW.

As a share of Germany’s total average daily consumption, this corresponds to approximately 35.78 % for Amprion, 30.16 % for TenneT, 21.75 % for 50Hertz, and 12.31 % for TransnetBW.

This leads to the clear ranking Amprion > TenneT > 50Hertz > TransnetBW.

Visualization 1 — Average Regional Consumption

This chart compares the average electricity consumption across Germany’s TSO control areas.

This bar chart compares the average daily electricity consumption of Germany’s four TSO control areas between 2022 and 2025. It highlights clear regional differences in the overall level of demand. Amprion shows the highest average consumption, followed by TenneT, while 50Hertz and especially TransnetBW are lower. The chart therefore emphasizes that electricity consumption is not evenly distributed across Germany, but differs substantially by region.

Visualization 2 — Regional Consumption Boxplot

This boxplot shows the distribution of electricity consumption across the different TSO regions.

This boxplot shows the distribution and variability of electricity consumption in each TSO control area. In contrast to the average bar chart, it does not only display the mean level, but also the spread, median, and typical range of the data. The plot confirms that the regions differ in their general consumption level, while also showing that fluctuations exist within each region. Overall, the ranking remains stable, with Amprion and TenneT above 50Hertz and TransnetBW.

Visualization 3 — Regional Consumption Time Series

This time series shows how electricity consumption develops over time in the different TSO regions.

This time series illustrates how electricity consumption developed over time in the four TSO control areas from 2022 to 2025. Although the regions differ in their absolute consumption level, the overall movement is quite similar: increases and decreases tend to occur in parallel. This suggests that the regional differences are mainly differences in scale rather than completely different regional dynamics. Weather conditions and holidays may have some influence, but their effect is not strong enough to create fundamentally different trends between the regions.

Interpretation

These regional differences can plausibly be explained by a combination of structural factors rather than by weather alone.

Regions with more industry and energy-intensive economic activity tend to have higher electricity demand. In the dataset, Amprion stands out clearly, which is consistent with a control area that includes major industrial and densely populated regions.

Population size and urban concentration are also likely to play an important role. This helps explain why TenneT and Amprion are above 50Hertz and TransnetBW.

In addition, regional demand structures differ. Some areas are more strongly shaped by industry, others by services, commercial activity or residential demand, which creates different overall consumption levels within the national power system.

Summary: Electricity consumption differs clearly across Germany’s TSO areas and appears to be driven mainly by regional economic structure, population size and demand composition.
