RQ7 — Renewable Generation and Electricity Prices

How are electricity prices related to renewable electricity generation levels, and do high renewable generation periods correspond to lower electricity prices in Germany?

Research Focus

This analysis examines whether electricity prices decrease when wind and photovoltaic generation are high.

Visualization 1 — Renewables versus Price Time Series

This chart shows how renewable generation and electricity prices develop over time.

Visualization 2 — Renewables versus Price Scatter

This scatter plot highlights the relationship between renewable generation levels and electricity prices.

Visualization 3 — Renewable Components and Price Correlation

This chart examines how different renewable components are associated with electricity prices.

Interpretation

The analysis indicates that electricity prices tend to be lower during periods with high renewable generation, especially when wind and photovoltaic output are strong.

This pattern is consistent with the idea that a higher supply of renewable electricity can reduce price pressure in the market.

Summary: Higher renewable generation is generally associated with lower electricity prices in Germany, although the exact relationship can vary depending on the market situation and the specific renewable components involved.
