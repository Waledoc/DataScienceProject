RQ2 — Seasonal Patterns in Electricity Consumption

How does electricity consumption vary across seasons in Germany, and how does this relate to seasonal weather conditions?

Research Focus

This analysis examines how electricity consumption changes across the seasons in Germany and how these patterns are related to temperature and other seasonal weather conditions.

Key Results

Across both visualizations, the results show a clear seasonal pattern in electricity consumption in Germany. Winter has the highest average electricity consumption, while summer has the lowest. Spring and autumn lie in between, with autumn slightly above spring.

In the seasonal chart, average electricity consumption is approximately 1.39 million MWh in winter, 1.28 million MWh in autumn, 1.26 million MWh in spring, and 1.19 million MWh in summer.

The regional scatter plot also shows a clear negative relationship between temperature and electricity consumption. Across all four TSO zones, higher maximum temperatures are generally associated with lower daily electricity consumption.

Overall, the results indicate that electricity consumption is highest in colder seasons and decreases as temperatures rise, with similar temperature-related patterns visible across the German TSO regions.

Visualization 1 — Seasonal Consumption and Weather in Germany

This chart shows seasonal electricity consumption and related weather conditions in Germany. At the bottom of the chart, we see the four seasons winter, spring, summer, and autumn. The left vertical scale shows average electricity consumption in MWh, while the right vertical scale shows the weather values for average temperature and sunshine duration. The chart shows that electricity consumption is highest in winter and lowest in summer, while temperature and sunshine peak in summer.

Visualization 2 — Regional Consumption versus Temperature

This scatter plot compares daily electricity consumption and maximum temperature across the four German TSO regions. The horizontal axis shows maximum temperature in °C, while the vertical scale shows daily consumption in MWh. Each point represents an observation, and the trend lines show that higher temperatures are generally linked to lower consumption in all four regions.

Interpretation

Electricity consumption in Germany shows a clear seasonal pattern. Consumption is highest in winter and lowest in summer, which matches the general temperature structure shown in the seasonal chart.

A likely explanation is that colder weather increases electricity demand through higher heating-related demand, more lighting demand during darker months, and generally higher seasonal energy use in households and parts of the economy.

The regional scatter plot supports this interpretation, because all four TSO regions show a negative relationship between maximum temperature and electricity consumption. This suggests that temperature is an important seasonal factor behind the observed consumption pattern.

Summary: Electricity consumption in Germany is clearly seasonal, with the highest demand in winter and the lowest in summer, and higher temperatures are generally associated with lower electricity consumption.
